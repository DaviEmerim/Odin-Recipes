Meng's Chinese Hotpot

The chinese hotpot is like a buffet on your dinner table. It's essentialy a broth (the hotpot) and mise en place ingredients (meat, veggies, pasta, etc.) to place in the broth. Everyone can have their own style of hotpot, so it's a meal you definitely have to try out! First of all, make sure you have a portable burner to place a pot on your dinning table

Ingredients for broth:

  • 500g of pork meat with bones, cut into large chunks
  • 1/2 tbsp cooking oil
  • 2 inches of ginger
  • 1/8 of an white onion
  • 1 tbsp of wine
  • 1/2 tbsp of salt
  • A little white pepper

Ingredients to put in the hot pot:

  • 300g of thin sliced frozen meat per person (you can ask the butcher to cut it)
  • 2 leaves of chinese leaves per person
  • 4 mushrooms per person
  • 1/4 of a pasta package per person

Cooking steps:

  1. Soak the pork meat in a pot of water for half an hour to draw out the mioglobins off the meat for a cleaner flavor
  2. Replace the water with cold one to cover the meat, boil it altogether and simmer for a couple minutes
  3. Discard the water and take the meat out to rinse it
  4. Add half table spoon of oil to the pot and fry the pork meat until the sides are brown
  5. Add 10 cups of hot boiling water to emulsify the fats and proteins from the meat
  6. Add 2 inches of smashed ginger, 1/8 of a white onion, and a tablespoon of wine
  7. Let it all simmer for 2 hours
  8. Add half tablespoon of salt and a sprinkle of white pepper
Congratulations, you did a hot pot broth! Now, just place the pot on the portable burner and place the ingredients to put in the hotpot around! Back to recipes
